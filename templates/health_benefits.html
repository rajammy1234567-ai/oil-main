Cold & Single Pressed Mustard Oil

Discover the ancient wisdom and modern science behind nature's golden elixir

The Traditional Method, Preserved

Cold & Single pressed mustard oil, extracted from mustard seeds through a traditional method without the use of heat or chemicals, is a staple in many Indian households.

Known for its pungent aroma, distinct flavor, and multiple health benefits, mustard oil has been used in cooking, skincare, and medicinal practices for centuries.

Cold-pressing helps retain the oil's natural properties, nutrients, and active compounds, making it a healthier choice compared to refined oils.

What Makes Cold & Single Pressed Mustard Oil Special?

Cold pressing involves extracting oil from mustard seeds without applying high heat, which helps preserve its vitamins, minerals, and essential fatty acids.

This method ensures that the oil retains its natural antioxidants and other beneficial compounds, such as omega-3 fatty acids, omega-6 fatty acids, vitamin E, and monounsaturated fats.

Unlike refined oils, which may lose their nutritional value during processing, Cold & Single pressed mustard oil remains nutrient-rich, making it a superior option for both culinary and medicinal purposes.

9 Powerful Health Benefits

Science-backed benefits for your wellbeing

1. May Promote Heart Health

Cold & Single pressed mustard oil is rich in monounsaturated and polyunsaturated fatty acids (MUFA and PUFA), particularly omega-3 and omega-6 fatty acids. These healthy fats play a crucial role in maintaining heart health by reducing bad cholesterol (LDL) levels and increasing good cholesterol (HDL) levels.

This balance of cholesterol levels helps prevent the buildup of plaque in the arteries, lowering the risk of heart disease, stroke, and high blood pressure. Additionally, the omega-3 fatty acids in mustard oil have anti-inflammatory properties that help protect the heart and reduce inflammation in blood vessels.

2. May Improve Digestive Health

Mustard oil is known to stimulate digestion by promoting the secretion of digestive juices and bile, which aid in breaking down food more efficiently. Its natural pungency also acts as an appetite stimulant, helping people with poor appetite or digestive issues.

Cold & Single pressed mustard oil has mild laxative properties, making it useful for preventing constipation and promoting regular bowel movements. By improving digestion and nutrient absorption, mustard oil contributes to better overall digestive health.

3. May Boost the Immune System

Cold & Single pressed mustard oil is a rich source of antioxidants, such as vitamin E and selenium, which help strengthen the immune system. These antioxidants neutralize harmful free radicals that can cause cellular damage and weaken immunity.

The antimicrobial and antifungal properties of mustard oil also make it effective in fighting infections, protecting the body from harmful bacteria, fungi, and viruses. By enhancing immunity, mustard oil helps the body stay resilient against common illnesses and infections.

4. May Enhance Skin Health

Cold & Single pressed mustard oil is a popular remedy in traditional skincare due to its rich content of vitamins, antioxidants, and essential fatty acids. It is known to nourish and hydrate the skin, making it soft, supple, and glowing.

Mustard oil's high vitamin E content protects the skin from environmental damage, such as pollution and UV rays, which can lead to premature aging and wrinkles. And its natural anti-inflammatory properties help soothe irritated skin, making it a great option for people with dry or sensitive skin.

5. May Support Hair Growth

Cold & Single pressed mustard oil is a well-known natural remedy for promoting hair growth and preventing hair loss. The oil is rich in essential fatty acids, vitamins, and minerals that nourish the hair follicles and scalp, promoting healthy hair growth.

Mustard oil also contains protein, which is essential for strong, thick hair. Its high vitamin E content helps prevent premature greying and hair damage caused by environmental factors. Massaging cold & single pressed mustard oil into the scalp increases blood circulation, which stimulates the hair follicles and encourages hair growth. The oil's antibacterial and antifungal properties also help combat dandruff and other scalp infections, keeping the scalp healthy and clean.

6. May Relieve Joint and Muscle Pain

Cold & Single pressed mustard oil is often used in traditional massage therapies to relieve joint and muscle pain. The oil contains omega-3 fatty acids, which have anti-inflammatory properties that can help reduce inflammation and pain in the joints.

Mustard oil is commonly used to massage people suffering from arthritis, rheumatism, and other inflammatory conditions. The oil's warming effect also helps improve blood circulation and relax muscles, providing relief from muscle stiffness and soreness.

7. May Aid in Respiratory Health

Cold & Single pressed mustard oil is a natural decongestant and can help relieve respiratory conditions such as asthma, bronchitis, and sinusitis. The pungent aroma of mustard oil helps clear the nasal passages and reduce congestion, making it easier to breathe.

Mustard oil can also be used as a natural remedy for colds and coughs by applying it to the chest or inhaling its vapors. The oil's anti-inflammatory properties help soothe irritated respiratory tissues, providing relief from coughing and wheezing.

8. May Promote Oral Health

In traditional practices, cold & single pressed mustard oil is used for oil pulling—a technique where the oil is swished around the mouth to improve oral hygiene. Mustard oil's antibacterial properties help kill harmful bacteria in the mouth, reducing the risk of cavities, gum disease, and bad breath.

The oil also helps strengthen the gums and teeth, promoting overall oral health. Oil pulling with mustard oil can also reduce inflammation in the gums and prevent bleeding.

9. Natural Detoxifier

Cold & Single pressed mustard oil acts as a natural detoxifier, helping to cleanse the body of toxins and impurities. The oil stimulates the liver and spleen, enhancing their detoxification functions.

Mustard oil's ability to improve digestion and promote healthy bowel movements also aids in the elimination of waste and toxins from the body. By supporting the body's natural detoxification processes, mustard oil helps maintain overall health and well-being.

How to Use Cold & Single Pressed Mustard Oil

Cooking

Mustard oil is widely used in Indian and South Asian cuisines for cooking and frying. Its strong flavor adds depth to dishes, and its high smoke point makes it ideal for deep frying and sautéing.

Massage

Mustard oil can be massaged into the skin to relieve pain, improve circulation, and promote relaxation. It is also used in scalp massages to stimulate hair growth and nourish the hair.

Skincare

Apply mustard oil directly to the skin to hydrate and nourish the skin. It can also be used as a natural remedy for acne, eczema, and other skin conditions.

Oil Pulling

Swish mustard oil in your mouth for 10-15 minutes as part of your oral hygiene routine to promote healthy teeth and gums.

Hair Care

Use mustard oil as a hair mask or scalp treatment to promote hair growth, prevent hair loss, and treat dandruff.

Precautions

While cold & single pressed mustard oil offers numerous benefits, it is essential to use it in moderation.

Excessive consumption can lead to adverse effects due to its erucic acid content, which has been linked to heart issues in some studies.

Pregnant women and individuals with existing health conditions should consult a healthcare professional before incorporating mustard oil into their diet.
